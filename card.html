Toucan Toco

The colorful and large bill, which in some large species measures more than half the length of the body, is the hallmark of toucans.

  • Scientific name: Ramphastos toco
  • Life: 50
  • Legth: 120cm
  • Habitat: Amazonian forest

The toco toucan has a striking plumage with a mainly black body, a white throat, chest and uppertail-coverts, and red undertail-coverts. What appears to be a blue iris is actually thin blue skin around the eye. This blue skin is surrounded by another ring of bare, orange skin. The most noticeable feature, however, is its huge bill, which measures from 15.8 to 23 cm in length, which is yellow-orange. It looks heavy, but as in other toucans it is relatively light because the inside largely is hollow. The tongue is nearly as long as the bill and very flat.
